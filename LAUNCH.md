## HN Post Body

Mao Zedong won against superior forces for 22 years. His collected works span 1,258 pages of military, political, and philosophical writing — and buried inside are thinking frameworks that apply directly to startups, product strategy, and organizational leadership.

I extracted 5 core frameworks:

1. **Contradiction Analysis** — Find the one tension driving everything, then resolve it with the right method
2. **Practice Theory** — Investigate reality, act, verify, repeat. (The Lean Startup cycle, 70 years early)
3. **Military Strategy** — You don't need more resources. Concentrate what you have. Force the opponent to react.
4. **Mass Line** — Your users know more than you. "From the masses, to the masses" is build-measure-learn.
5. **United Front** — Win the middle forces. Isolate the extremes. Keep your independence.

Each framework has:
- English + Chinese explanations
- Original text excerpts
- Application templates (copy-paste prompts for Claude/ChatGPT)
- An [interactive CheatSheet](assets/cheatsheet.html) with one-click prompts

The repo also has 214 chapter summaries across all 5 volumes, organized by period (1925-1957).

**Not a political statement.** The frameworks are extracted for analytical value — the same way people study Sun Tzu's Art of War without commanding armies.

Every complex situation has one tension driving everything else. Find it. Resolve it with the right method. Classic mistake: treating symptoms instead of the root contradiction.

3/ **2. Practice Theory**
"Theory without practice is empty. Practice without theory is blind." The cycle: investigate → act → verify → repeat. Same loop as build-measure-learn, 70 years before Eric Ries.

"From the masses, to the masses." Gather scattered feedback → find the pattern → return it as actionable guidance. Your users already know what they need. You just need to listen systematically.

Win the middle forces. Isolate the extremes. Keep your independence. "Make more friends and fewer enemies." Coalition-building for founders, PMs, and anyone navigating organizational politics.
