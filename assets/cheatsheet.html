Prompt 1: Competitive Strategy Analysis

I'm a startup competing against a well-funded incumbent (or describe your situation). Use Mao's military strategy framework: 1. Map the balance of forces 2. Where can I achieve 10:1 local superiority? 3. What's my protracted war strategy: defense -> stalemate -> counter-offensive? 4. How do I force the incumbent to react to me instead of vice versa?

Prompt 2: Complex Problem Analysis (Contradiction Method)

Apply Mao's contradiction analysis to this situation: [Describe your problem here] 1. List all contradictions present 2. Identify the principal contradiction 3. Which aspect currently dominates? 4. What conditions would flip it? 5. What specific method of resolution does this specific contradiction require?

Prompt 3: Coalition Building (United Front)

I need to build a coalition for [project/initiative]. Use Mao's united front framework: 1. Map all stakeholders: core allies, middle forces, temporary allies, hard opposition 2. How do I win over the middle forces (the swing vote)? 3. What are my red lines where I maintain independence? 4. How do I isolate rather than confront the hardest opposition?

Prompt 4: Leadership Decision (Mass Line + Practice)

I'm facing a difficult team decision. Apply Mao's mass line + practice theory: 1. How do I investigate the real situation (not my assumptions)? 2. From the scattered feedback, what's the pattern? 3. What's the concrete action plan? 4. How do I verify the results and iterate?
