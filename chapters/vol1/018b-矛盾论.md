# 矛盾论
*On Contradiction*

**Date:** August 1937 | 一九三七年八月
**Significance:** Philosophical magnum opus — paired with *On Practice* as twin pillars of Mao's epistemology

---

## Key Argument

Systematic exposition of dialectical materialism as a practical analytical method. Mao transforms abstract Hegelian-Marxist dialectics into an operational toolkit organized around six interconnected investigations:

1. **Two Worldviews** — metaphysical (static, isolated) vs. dialectical (dynamic, interconnected). Internal contradictions are the fundamental cause of development; external conditions are secondary.
2. **Universality** — contradiction exists in all things, throughout their entire development. No state is without contradiction.
3. **Particularity** — each contradiction has specific character. *The method of resolution must match the specific nature of the contradiction.* This is the practical heart of the essay.
4. **Principal Contradiction** — in complex situations, one contradiction dominates. Resolving it unlocks everything else.
5. **Principal Aspect** — within each contradiction, one aspect dominates. When the dominant aspect flips, the nature of the thing changes qualitatively.
6. **Identity & Struggle** — opposites presuppose and transform into each other (identity is conditional); they simultaneously push against each other (struggle is absolute).

**Core operational principle:** "Concrete analysis of concrete conditions." (具体地分析具体的情况) — the living soul of Marxism.

---

## Structure

### I. Two Worldviews / 两种宇宙观
- Metaphysical: static, isolated, external causes only. Cannot explain qualitative change.
- Dialectical: interconnected, dynamic, driven by internal contradictions.
- **Key:** Internal cause is fundamental (内因是根本); external cause is the condition (外因是条件).

### II. Universality / 矛盾的普遍性
- Contradiction in ALL things, through ALL stages of development.
- If you see no contradiction, you have not looked deeply enough.

### III. Particularity / 矛盾的特殊性
- Different qualities require different methods of resolution.
- Must study: form of motion, developmental stage, each aspect, their totality.
- **Method:** Concrete analysis of concrete conditions.

### IV. Principal Contradiction / 主要的矛盾
- Among many contradictions, one is always principal.
- It determines or influences all others.
- **Operational:** List all contradictions -> find which one drives the rest -> focus there.

### V. Principal Aspect / 矛盾的主要方面
- Within each contradiction, aspects are unequal.
- The principal aspect determines the nature of the thing.
- When it flips, the nature changes qualitatively.

### VI. Identity and Struggle / 同一性和斗争性
- **Identity:** opposites presuppose each other; can transform into each other under conditions. Relative, conditional.
- **Struggle:** constant mutual opposition and exclusion. Absolute, unconditional.

### VII. Antagonism / 对抗在矛盾中的地位
- Some contradictions are antagonistic (explosive resolution); others non-antagonistic.
- Misidentifying one as the other is catastrophic.

## Key Quotes / 金句

> 事物的矛盾法则，即对立统一的法则，是唯物辩证法的最根本的法则。

*The law of contradiction in things, that is, the law of the unity of opposites, is the fundamental law of materialist dialectics.*

---

> 马克思主义的最本质的东西，马克思主义的活的灵魂，就在于具体地分析具体的情况。

*The most essential thing in Marxism, the living soul of Marxism, is the concrete analysis of concrete conditions.*

---

> 不同质的矛盾，只有用不同质的方法才能解决。

*Qualitatively different contradictions can only be resolved by qualitatively different methods.*

---

## Application Template / 应用模板

1. **MAP** all contradictions in the situation
2. **CLASSIFY** each as antagonistic or non-antagonistic
3. **IDENTIFY** the principal contradiction
4. **DETERMINE** which aspect of the principal contradiction dominates
5. **ANALYZE** the specific character of this contradiction
6. **MATCH** the method of resolution to the specific character
7. **IDENTIFY** conditions under which the dominant aspect would flip
8. **ACT**, observe results, iterate
