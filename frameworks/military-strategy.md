# Military Strategy · 军事战略

> *"Strategy is the study of the laws of a war situation as a whole."*
> *"战略问题是研究战争全局的规律的东西。"*

## Core Principles

### 1. Concentration of Forces · 集中兵力

**"十倍于敌"** — Concentrate absolute superiority against a single enemy point. Spread thin, you lose everywhere. Concentrate, you win decisively.

**Competitive Application:**
- Don't compete on all fronts simultaneously
- Pick one beachhead, commit overwhelming resources
- "伤其十指不如断其一指" — Wounding ten fingers is not as effective as cutting off one

### 2. Active Defense · 积极防御

Defense is not passivity — it's preparation for counter-attack. **Defend to preserve strength, attack to destroy the enemy.** Pure defense leads to defeat.

**Application:** When defending market position, always maintain an offensive capability. Defense buys time for the next attack.

### 3. Strategic Withdrawal · 战略退却

Retreat when the enemy advances; harass when he halts; attack when he tires; pursue when he withdraws. **The 16-character formula:**

> 敌进我退，敌驻我扰，敌疲我打，敌退我追
> Enemy advances, we retreat; enemy halts, we harass; enemy tires, we attack; enemy retreats, we pursue.

**Application:** Know when to cede ground. Preserve strength for favorable engagements.

### 4. Protracted War · 持久战

Against a stronger opponent, victory comes through **three stages**:
1. **Strategic defensive** — Survive, preserve strength
2. **Strategic stalemate** — Bleed the opponent, build your own forces  
3. **Strategic counter-offensive** — Strike decisively

**Application:** When competing against a dominant incumbent, plan for a long game. Don't expect quick victory.

### 5. Initiative · 主动权

**"行动自由是军队的命脉"** — Freedom of action is the lifeblood of an army. Force the opponent to react to you, not vice versa.

**Application:** Set the agenda. Make competitors respond to your moves.

## Strategic Checklist · 战略检查清单

```
□ What is the overall balance of forces? (敌我力量对比)
□ Where can I achieve local superiority? (局部优势)
□ What is my line of retreat? (退却路线)
□ Where is the enemy's weak point? (薄弱环节)
□ Am I forcing reactions or reacting? (主动权在谁手里)
□ Is this a short campaign or a protracted war? (速决战还是持久战)
```

## Key Concepts

| Concept | Chinese | Meaning |
|---------|---------|---------|
| Interior lines | 内线作战 | Operating within enemy encirclement — use mobility to create local superiority |
| Exterior lines | 外线作战 | Attacking from outside — divide and encircle |
| Annihilation warfare | 歼灭战 | Destroy enemy units completely, don't just drive them off |
| Mobile warfare | 运动战 | Fight on the move; avoid static positions against a stronger foe |
